Curiosity
The Curiosity rover is a robotic exploration vehicle developed by NASA to investigate the surface of Mars. Launched on November 26, 2011, it successfully landed on Mars on August 6, 2012. Curiosity is a part of the Mars Science Laboratory (MSL) mission and is primarily tasked with searching for signs of past or present life, as well as studying the geology and climate of Mars.

Opportunity
Opportunity was one of NASA's twin Mars Exploration Rovers, alongside Spirit. Launched on July 7, 2003, and landing on January 25, 2004, Opportunity was designed for a primary mission of 90 Martian sols (Martian days), but it far exceeded expectations, operating for over 14 years. Unfortunately, the rover's mission officially concluded on February 13, 2019, as it lost communication with Earth during a massive dust storm on Mars.

Spirit
The Spirit rover was a robotic exploration vehicle developed by NASA as part of the Mars Exploration Rover mission. It was launched on June 10, 2003, and successfully landed on Mars on January 3, 2004. Spirit's primary mission was to study the geology and climate of Mars, as well as to search for evidence of past water on the planet's surface. The Spirit mission officially concluded on May 25, 2011, when it lost communication with Earth.
